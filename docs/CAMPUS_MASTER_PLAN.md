# ORVEX Fundación DR Campus Master Plan

This document is the controlling physical-program specification for the ORVEX Fundación DR national headquarters campus.

## Master headquarters building

The principal headquarters building must contain four floors.

- Floor area per floor: approximately 100,000 square feet
- Total headquarters building area: approximately 400,000 square feet
- Floors 1 through 3: executive leadership, administration, beneficiary operations, finance, compliance, technology, AI, case management, logistics coordination, training, meetings, and other core foundation functions
- Floor 4: Employee Services Center, fully dedicated to employees

The former three-floor, 300,000-square-foot headquarters concept is superseded by this four-floor, 400,000-square-foot requirement.

## Fourth floor — Employee Services Center

The entire fourth floor must be reserved for employee-only services. Public visitors and beneficiaries must not have unrestricted access. Access must be controlled by employee credentials and appropriate visitor authorization.

The floor must be planned as a complete employee-support environment and should include, at minimum:

- Full-service beauty salon
- Barber shop
- Employee primary-care and occupational-health clinic
- Dental services
- Pharmacy or medication-dispensing support, subject to applicable licensing
- Mental-health, counseling, and employee-assistance services
- Fitness center and gym
- Wellness, yoga, stretching, and rehabilitation rooms
- Large employee cafeteria and dining areas
- Coffee shop and healthy snack service
- Convenience store or employee essentials shop
- Banking, ATM, or approved financial-service access
- Human Resources service center
- Benefits and payroll assistance desks
- Employee training classrooms
- Computer learning and digital-skills center
- Library, quiet study, and reading lounges
- General break rooms and relaxation lounges
- Prayer, reflection, and meditation rooms
- Childcare and daycare center, subject to licensing and safeguarding requirements
- Lactation and parent-support rooms
- Uniform issue, fitting, and replacement services
- Laundry, dry-cleaning, tailoring, and alteration services
- Employee mail, package, and internal pickup center
- Locker rooms, showers, changing rooms, and personal-storage lockers
- Bicycle storage and commuter-support facilities
- Recreation and game rooms
- Small theater, presentation, and employee-event space
- Employee recognition and awards area
- Indoor garden, quiet zones, and restorative spaces
- Accessibility accommodations throughout
- Security, reception, facilities support, cleaning, storage, and back-of-house areas required to operate the floor

## Design principles

The fourth floor must be designed around employee dignity, health, convenience, retention, productivity, and work-life support.

Required principles:

- Employee-only zoning and access control
- Universal accessibility
- Medical privacy and confidential counseling rooms
- Child safeguarding and controlled daycare access
- Food-safety and healthcare licensing compliance
- Adequate elevators, stairs, emergency exits, and evacuation capacity for the full four-floor building
- Proper ventilation, acoustic separation, sanitation, fire protection, and infection-control considerations
- Separation of clinical, childcare, food-service, fitness, salon, and general office functions where required
- Flexible spaces that can be reconfigured as employee needs change
- Integration with the campus renewable-energy, water, communications, security, and emergency systems

## Software and operational implications

Where the ORVEX Fundación DR software supports campus operations, Codex must account for the fourth-floor services through appropriate modules or integrations, including:

- Employee eligibility and credential-based access
- Service appointments and scheduling
- Clinic and occupational-health workflows with strict medical-data permissions
- Salon and barber appointments
- Daycare enrollment, authorized pickup, attendance, and safeguarding controls
- Cafeteria access, menus, dietary accommodations, and employee meal programs
- Fitness and wellness enrollment
- Facility capacity, room booking, and maintenance requests
- Uniform, laundry, tailoring, locker, package, and employee-service requests
- Service usage reporting that protects employee privacy
- Audit logs for sensitive access and administrative changes

The system must not expose employee medical, counseling, childcare, or benefits information outside authorized roles.
